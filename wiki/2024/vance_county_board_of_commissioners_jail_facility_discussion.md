# Vance County Board of Commissioners Jail Facility Discussion

**Date:** April 15, 2024  
**Year:** 2024  
**Type:** Board of Commissioners Meeting Minutes

## Summary

The Vance County Board of Commissioners held a work session to discuss the future of the county jail. They reviewed a feasibility study outlining costs for renovating the existing jail versus constructing a new facility. Sheriff Curtis Brame advised against renovating due to extensive repairs needed. The board considered regionalization and potential funding sources, agreeing on the necessity of a new facility while maintaining the current one.

## Key Decisions

- Agreed on the need for a new jail facility
- Instructed the Sheriff to pursue a maintenance contract for the current jail

## Action Items

- [ ] Commissioner Sean A. Alston, Sr. to contact Washington for potential funding
- [ ] Sheriff Curtis Brame to contact H.G. Reynolds for a maintenance contract

## Topics Discussed

- Jail renovation vs. new construction
- Feasibility study review
- Current jail conditions
- Regionalization
- Inmate length of stay
- Funding sources

## Budget & Financial Matters

- Renovate existing jail: $5.3M
- Renovate with addition: $31.3M
- New 250-bed facility: $42.6M
- Annual payments for new jail: $3.2M (13¢ tax increase)
- Annual payments for renovation with addition: $2.4M (8¢ tax increase)
