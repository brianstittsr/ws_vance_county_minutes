# Vance County Board of Commissioners Work Session on Recruitment, Retention, and Opioid Committee

**Date:** August 18, 2025  
**Year:** 2025  
**Type:** Board of Commissioners Meeting Minutes

## Summary

The Vance County Board of Commissioners held a work session to address key issues including the recruitment and retention of law enforcement officers and the establishment of an opioid committee. Sheriff Curtis Brame highlighted the challenge of losing deputies to other agencies due to lower salaries and proposed an increase in the minimum salary. The board discussed developing a progression and incentive plan. Additionally, the board agreed on the composition of a new opioid committee to address the ongoing crisis.

## Key Decisions

- Agreement on the composition of the opioid committee
- Consensus on the need to develop a salary progression and incentive plan for law enforcement

## Action Items

- [ ] County Manager C. Renee Perry to develop a plan for sworn officers and present it in November
- [ ] Sheriff Curtis Brame to provide current deputy applications and certifications to the County Manager
- [ ] Staff to research potential grants for law enforcement salary support

## Topics Discussed

- Sheriff’s Office Recruitment and Retention
- Opioid Committee establishment

## Budget & Financial Matters

- Discussion on increasing the minimum salary for deputies from $46,266 to $55,000
